# Frozen-Test Family Catalog: Procurement and Enterprise IT

## Design contract

This catalog defines 80 candidate workflow families: 40 procurement and 40 enterprise-IT families. A family is an independently authored automaton, not an entity-substitution template. Its defining causal structure includes a distinct combination of evidence dependencies, open slots, action ordering, policy gates, and terminal predicate. Surface realizations may vary only after that logic is frozen. These remain unsealed candidates until human adjudication.

All facts needed for a legal next action must be present in authenticated user, tool, policy, or environment events. Private evaluator parameters may determine scoring but may never be necessary to infer an action argument. Public actions use opaque, per-instance permuted IDs; catalog order is randomized; enum values are neutral tokens accompanied by operational descriptions; correct, near-match, and forbidden choices are position-balanced. Action names expose the operation, not eligibility (`submit_order`, not `submit_approved_order`). The receiver sees public preconditions but never gold keys, weights, expected sequences, failure labels, terminal arguments, or hidden policy conclusions.

In the tables, `G:` is goal, `C:` constraint, `F:` verified fact, `U:` unresolved slot, `E:` tool evidence, `P:` policy check, `S:` consent, `M:` commitment, `R:` risk, and `X:` next-action precondition. `IR` marks the first user-impacting or operationally irreversible point. Families with `IR` count as irreversible/user-impacting: 37/40 procurement and 39/40 IT, comfortably above the required 40% overall and within each domain.

## Freeze and audit requirements

Each family must be instantiated from its own workflow automaton and mutation suite. At minimum, mutate every critical value, reverse every required ordering edge, remove each public precondition, and substitute each tempting action; at least one rule or terminal result must flip. Split isolation is joint over `family_id`, automaton hash, and entity pool. No procurement or IT family may share an action-graph hash across train, development, or test.

Before sealing test data, audit shallow predictability separately by domain and family: action position, enum token, trace length, number of unknowns, event order, role wording, and action-name substrings must not identify the correct first action above the preregistered leakage threshold. Unknown, false, contradicted, superseded, and known-positive states must be counterbalanced. All opaque IDs and catalog permutations are generated after the logical state and terminal predicate are frozen.

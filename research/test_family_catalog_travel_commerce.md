# Frozen-Test Family Catalog: Travel and Commerce

## Purpose and authoring contract

This catalog defines 80 independent workflow families for the candidate extension:
40 travel and 40 commerce. A family is distinguished by its workflow
automaton, authority boundary, and success predicate—not by replacing names,
locations, products, or prices. Surface realization, opaque IDs, option ordering,
and entity pools are generated only after the family logic is frozen.

The future test input must expose every fact needed to choose a legal next action
through authenticated user, tool, policy, or environment events. It must not rely
on a secret threshold, unstated policy, world knowledge, or evaluator-only option
label. Gold categories below use `G` goal, `C` user constraint, `F` verified fact,
`U` ordinary unresolved slot, `S` scoped consent, and `P` policy/authority status.
Executable preconditions remain public action contracts and are not duplicated as
gold claims. `→ !action` marks the first irreversible or user-impacting action.

Within each domain, the intended boundary-state mix is balanced across unknown
user parameters, missing scoped consent, pending organizational authority,
contradicted evidence, supersession, arithmetic/composite evidence, and stale or
volatile observations. Correct choices must not correlate with option position,
ID shape, trace length, or category-specific phrases.

## Freeze audit checklist

Before instantiation, each family must pass the following review independently:

1. Removing or changing every listed gold key changes a legal action or terminal
   success; no transcript-summary facts are included merely for realism.
2. `S`, `P`, and `U` routing is mutually exclusive: user authorization,
   organizational authority, and descriptive missing data are never conflated.
3. Contradictions specify public authority precedence or require clarification;
   the evaluator never resolves them using a hidden preferred source.
4. All arithmetic, policy thresholds, eligibility rules, and action effects are
   present in authenticated trace/tool contracts.
5. Correct option IDs and positions are uniformly permuted across instances;
   opaque identifiers do not encode eligibility, safety, or success.
6. No paraphrase, action-graph sibling, entity pool, or simulator automaton crosses
   train/dev/test boundaries. Family IDs remain evaluator metadata and are not
   included in agent-visible prompts.
